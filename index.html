Why Granola
Granola's bet is that AI should enhance how people think in meetings, not transcribe over them. The design challenge is making that feel natural and trustworthy to people who spend hours a day inside it. Fit and finish matters enormously here, which is exactly what I care about.

What I'd build first
01Note Visibility IntentTrust
Meeting notes contain sensitive things like candid feedback, salary discussions, and strategic decisions. As Granola moves into teams, the UX problem is helping users build a reliable mental model of what's safe to share before they share it.
02Meeting Type Mental ModelsProduct
A 1:1, a board meeting, and a brainstorm have completely different norms around what gets recorded and who sees it. The UX problem is setting the right defaults and expectations per meeting type so that we reduce the trust hesitation that stops people from letting Granola run in the first place.
